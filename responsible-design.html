Responsible design

Role: Concept developer, physical prototype, interaction designer

Partner: Tommy Feldt

Scope: 4 weeks, halftime

ABOUT THE PROJECT

Sustainable interaction design is a growing research field which has it focus mostly on energy and how to decrease its negative impact on the planet. The hi-tech/lo-tech playground concept is a re-design of a traditional playground that allows children to explore and learn about different energy-related activities such as the collection, keeping, sharing and activation of energy, through physical play and collaboration. The playground design is divided into two halves, one lo-tech and one hi-tech. The lo-tech half has traditional playground items like swings, see-saws and bicycles, modified to let children generate electricity using their bodies. The electricity is then transported to the power pylon, a central energy storage. At the power pylon, the children can decide to use the electricity that they have generated to power two different interactive playground installations at the hi-tech half. They can, however, only play as long as they have enough energy â€“ when it runs out, the hi-tech half shuts down. We hope our design can help children to start thinking about their energy consumption from an early age. We discuss the balance between realism and immersion, the possibilities of learning through play and the impact of our design.
